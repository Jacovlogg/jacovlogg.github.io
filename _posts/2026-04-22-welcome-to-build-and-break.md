## What this blog is

Build & Break is a place for thinking out loud about software. Not polished
essays — more like a working notebook. Posts here will cover things I'm figuring
out as I go: architectural decisions, tradeoffs I've run into, systems I've tried
to understand or redesign.

The name says it: you build something, you find out where it breaks, and you
learn from that. I'm not interested in pretending I had the right answer from the
start.

## Why I'm writing

Writing forces clarity. When I can't explain something in plain text, it usually
means I don't understand it as well as I thought.

I also learn better when there's a record. Notes I can revisit, revise, and push
back against. Public writing adds a small amount of accountability — it's harder
to hand-wave when someone else might read it.

This isn't meant to be a portfolio or a brand. It's closer to a lab journal.

## What I'll write about

Mostly:

- **Software architecture** — how systems are structured, why certain patterns
  emerge, and when they become a liability
- **Tradeoffs** — the decisions that don't have a clean right answer, and how
  to reason about them honestly
- **Systems thinking** — how components interact, how complexity accumulates,
  and how to work with that rather than against it
- **Ideas in progress** — things I'm not sure about yet, questions more than answers

Expect posts to be updated. Expect some of them to be wrong. That's the point.
